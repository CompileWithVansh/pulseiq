Vitals & Blood Work

Values from your last blood test or routine checkup report

In mm Hg — the top number on your BP reading taken while sitting at rest. Normal range: 90–120. Found on any BP report or home BP monitor.
In mg/dl — total cholesterol from your blood test (lipid profile report). Desirable: below 200. Borderline high: 200–239. High: 240+.
Highest heart rate (bpm) reached during exercise or a stress test. If you don't know, estimate using: 220 − your age. E.g. age 45 → enter 175.
Fasting blood sugar = glucose level after not eating for 8+ hours. Found on your blood test report under "FBS" or "Fasting Glucose". Normal is below 100 mg/dl; diabetic range is above 126 mg/dl. If you are diabetic or your FBS is above 120, select Yes.

Clinical Evaluation

ECG and stress test findings — if you haven't had these tests, use the default (first) option for each field

ECG (Electrocardiogram) records the electrical activity of your heart. Result is printed on your ECG report. If you've never had an ECG, select Normal.
Angina is chest discomfort caused by reduced blood flow to the heart during physical effort. If you can exercise, climb stairs, or walk briskly without any chest discomfort, select No.
A numeric value from your stress test or exercise ECG report, labeled as "ST depression" or "Oldpeak". Measures how much the ST segment dips during exercise vs. rest. Range: 0–6.2. If you have no stress test report, enter 0.
The direction of the ST segment on your exercise ECG / stress test report. If you haven't had a stress test, select Upsloping (normal default).
From a coronary angiography or cardiac catheterization report — shows how many of the 3 main heart arteries have significant narrowing. If you haven't had this procedure, select 0.
From a nuclear stress test (thallium/sestamibi scan) or thalassemia blood disorder screening. This test shows how well blood reaches different parts of your heart. If you haven't had this test, select Normal.
